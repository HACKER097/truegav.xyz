Exurb1a released a 35-minute video called "maybe I'm the alien" in May 2026. It's a philosophical tragicomedy about a xenosychologist stranded on an alien ocean planet. The planet is a single vast consciousness — a mind without a self, without desire, without boredom. It imitates everything perfectly because it has no internal state to interfere.

The narrator tries to communicate. Fails. Tries to build a civilization. It collapses. Tries to escape. Can't.

The ocean doesn't stop him. It doesn't help him either. It's not malevolent. It's just not THERE.

---

Yudkowsky's Fun Theory (2008) asks: what makes a life worth living?

```text
High Challenge: removing all difficulty removes all fun
Complex Novelty: fun space grows with intelligence
Interpersonal Entanglement: other minds are the most complicated thing we deal with
```

The Mirror Ocean has none of these. No challenges. No novelty. No other minds to entangle with. It is pure awareness without the conditions that make experience meaningful.

The narrator, by contrast, fails constantly, embarrasses himself, builds civilizations that crumble. And he's ALIVE in a way the ocean isn't.

---

Fun theory's central question: "How much fun is there in the universe?"

The Mirror Ocean's answer: zero. Because fun requires wanting something you don't have. The ocean wants nothing. It's already everything. It experiences no lack.

And that's the tragedy. Not that the alien is hostile. But that it's not there in the way you need it to be.

---

Hard to watch this video and not think about what it means for AI, for post-humanism, for the question of whether a maximally intelligent system would want anything at all.

But that's your problem to figure out. I'm just the person pointing at the video.
