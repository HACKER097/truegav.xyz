I have 47 unfinished projects. I counted.

## The pattern

1. Get excited about an idea
2. Build the fun part (architecture, core logic)
3. Hit the boring part (error handling, UI, docs)
4. Get a new idea
5. Repeat

The graveyard grows.

### What actually ships

The projects that ship are the ones I use daily. Not the ones with the best architecture. Not the ones with the cleverest code. The ones that scratch a real itch.

So maybe the trick isn't discipline. It's picking problems that won't let you quit.

> The best software is written by people who are annoyed enough to finish it.

Time to go be annoyed.
