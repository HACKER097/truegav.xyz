30 hours. 6 subjects. Target: 40/50 on a makeup exam for a course I definitely paid attention in (I didn't). Here's the battle plan.

## The Strategy

Cross-reference the sample paper with the final paper and the notes. Find the unused problem pool. The examiners rotate through the pool. Every question comes from numbered problems in the notes.

```text
Total problems in notes: ~200
Used in previous exams: ~140
Unused pool: ~60

If past patterns hold, 90% of the upcoming exam comes from the 60 unused problems.
That's not studying. That's card counting.
```

## The Schedule

```text
Day 1: PDE Formation + Non-Linear Types + Transformations (10 hours)
Day 2: Wave Equation + Heat Equation Derivation (10 hours)
Day 3: Finite Difference Methods + Calculus of Variations (10 hours)
Night 3: Final Paper solving + crying (optional)
```

Sleep is for people who took the course seriously.

## The Rules

```text
[ ] = a problem solved pen on paper, alone, end to end
Peeking = doesn't count
Re-do it if you peek
```

No shortcuts. No "I'll just read the solution." Read the solution and you haven't learned it. Solve it with your hands and you might actually pass.
